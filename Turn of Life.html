Turn of Life


Board Game to Address Gender Issues
Project Aim

The goal of this board game is allowing participants to experience how people with different gender would encounter in the society.

Type

Gender and Womens Studies coursework

Role

This is a card-based strategy game and we discussed the rules and cards together. I was responsible for the design of the manual and scoreboard.

Team

6 members

Duration

2 months, April 2014 - June 2014

Concept

Although the society in Taiwan is comparatively gender-friendly, gender equality is still absent under many contexts and many people ignore or go against feminism. We started to think how to present these gender issues to people in a more familiar and simple way.
So, we decided to create a board game. It can not only provide the playfulness, but also help players to understand the limitations and restrains faced by people with different gender and other related issues such as social expectations and patriachal dividend.

Game Mechanics

1. Participants are assigned to different genders (male/female) and sexual orientations (homosexual/heterosexual).

2. Each participant picks two Life Goal Cards (with score criteria) randomly.

3. Start playing. In every round, participants have to make choices and get points.

4. In the end, the winners are who fulfill the score criteria from one of the Life Goal Cards.

Evaluation and Reflection

Background Settings

We assumed the background of this game is a patriarchal and heterosexual society. In this case, players can only experience the gender supressions, but could not envision how the society will be if it is gender-friendly.

Limited Depiction of Specific Groups

Although we added the possible situations that some vulnerable groups especially homosexual community would encounter with, some missed perspectives should be added such as gender bullying in school and gender discrimination in work place.

Intersection of Social Identities

Gender as one of the social identity strongly influences our lives. At the same time, other social identities such as social classes are important factors as well. For example, women in working class would have totally different life experiences from women in middle class.
